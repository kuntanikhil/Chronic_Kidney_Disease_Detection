Algorithms and Their Purpose

This page explains how each algorithm was used in the project pipeline, why it was selected, and what role it plays in kidney disease risk prediction.

Random Forest Classifier

Primary Prediction Model

Random Forest was selected for strong performance on mixed tabular health attributes and for robustness against overfitting by averaging many decision trees.

  • Handles nonlinear relationships well.
  • Works reliably with noisy clinical data.
  • Gives stable classification for CKD risk output.

XGBoost (Experimental Track)

Accuracy Optimization

XGBoost was considered during model experimentation to capture complex boundaries through gradient boosting and improve precision on difficult samples.

  • Boosted trees correct previous model mistakes.
  • Effective for structured medical datasets.
  • Useful for benchmarking against Random Forest.

Decision Tree (Baseline Model)

Interpretability Reference

Decision Tree helps as a transparent baseline to understand feature splits and compare explainability against ensemble methods.

  • Easy to interpret and visualize.
  • Supports quick baseline performance checks.
  • Highlights important clinical split rules.

Pipeline Perspective

End-to-End Purpose

The project combines data cleaning, feature encoding, model comparison, and production-serving to provide a practical major-project deployment flow in Flask.

  • Input collection through validated form fields.
  • Feature vector alignment to training schema.
  • Immediate prediction rendering in a user-friendly result view.

How Each Algorithm Is Used in Workflow

1. Data Preparation Categorical health fields are encoded into model-friendly numeric form.
2. Model Training Random Forest and other candidate algorithms are trained and compared.
3. Model Selection Best-performing model is selected for reliability on CKD classification.
4. Deployment Saved model is loaded in Flask and used by the /predict endpoint.
